The thesis

Growth shouldn't require an army.

Every ambitious company eventually hits the same wall: the goals demand more capacity than the team can deliver, and the obvious answer — hire — is slow, expensive, and a bet you can't easily unwind. So work piles onto the few people you have, the founder most of all, and momentum stalls at the edge of everyone's bandwidth.

The constraint is almost never effort. It's leverage. Give a small team the right systems — defined processes, reusable playbooks, automation that does the repetitive heavy lifting — and its effective output multiplies without a single new salary. That is the entire discipline of this firm.
